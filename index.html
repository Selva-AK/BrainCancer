Brain Cancer Prediction Using Random Forest, SVM, and Naive Bayes Classifiers
This project presents three differentmachine learning classification models to predict malignant orbenign nature of brain tumour. Support Vector Machine (SVM),Random forest, and Naive Bayes classifiers are implemented usingPython. Models are trained and validated on dataset obtainedfrom Surveillance Epidemiology and End ResultsPerformance of each classifier is evaluated andcompared to other two classification models in terms of precision,recall and F-score. A tool is designed using classifier providingbest performance on training dataset to predict malignant/benignnature of tumour for a test dataset provided as input to tool.

Abstract

Brain cancer is a rare type of cancer.The survival rate of a person suffering from brain cancer is stronglydependent on the type of tumormalignant or benign. Survival ofpatient rapidly decreases if tumor is malignant and has spreadbeyond primary location. Prediction of nature of tumor canhelp physicians to decide between targeted treatments for patient.Machine learning models are being widely utilized to improvethe prognosis of cancer. This project presents three differentmachine learning classification models to predict the malignant orbenign nature of brain tumor. Support vector machine,random forest, and naive bayes classifiers are implemented usingpython. Models are trained and validated on dataset obtainedfrom Surveillance epidemiology and end results foryears. Performance of each classifier is evaluated andcompared to the other two classification models in terms of precision,recall and F-score. A tool is designed using classifier providingbest performance on training dataset to predict malignant/benignnature of tumor for a test dataset provided as input to tool.Management of brain tumors is based on clinical and radiological information with presumed grade dictating treatment. Hence, a non-invasive assessment of tumor grade is of paramountimportance to choose the best treatment plan. convolutional neural networks represent oneof the effective deep learning-based techniques that have been used for brain tumor diagnosis. However, they are unable to handle input modifications effectively. Capsule neural networks are a novel type of machine learning architecture that was recently developed toaddress the drawbacks resistant to rotations and affine translations, which isbeneficial when processing medical imaging datasets. moreover, vision transformers-basedsolutions have been very recently proposed to address the issue of long-range dependency. This survey provides a comprehensive overview of brain tumor classification and segmentationtechniques, with a focus techniques. Thesurvey highlights the fundamental contributions of recent studies and the performance of state-of-the-the art techniques. Moreover, we present an in-depth discussion of crucial issues and open challenges.We also identify some key limitations and promising future research directions. We envisage that thissurvey shall serve as a good springboard for further study.
